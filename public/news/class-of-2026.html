CLASS OF 2026:
Champions Returning, Rookies Arriving,
Forty Years in the Rearview.

An inside look at the 23-driver grid lining up for VMRA's 40th anniversary season. The defending champ with a target on his back. The three-time champion looking for one more. The rookie class bringing new car numbers to a club that's raced for four decades. Here's what to watch, and who to watch it on.

By The VMRA Desk April 21, 2026

Forty seasons after the first VMRA race at Spanaway Speedway in 1986, the 2026 grid is the kind of roster that tells you exactly where a club is in its life cycle. You have a defending champion entering his prime, a veteran field with three former title winners still turning laps, a rookie class that just showed up with fresh car numbers, and, for the first time in years, a full slate of confirmed entries for every round on the tour. For a 40 year old club, that is not a coincidence. That is a sign the sport still has a pulse.

The 2026 season opener at Tri-City Raceway on April 12 gave the field its first chance to show form. Defending champion Kyten Jones won on a broken upper control arm, a result that has already entered the club's folklore. But the field closed to within seconds of him, and this weekend at Evergreen is where the real championship picture begins.

Before the pack lines up on the Superspeedway of the West, let's meet them.

§ 05 What to Watch Across 11 Rounds

The 2026 calendar runs from April through September, spanning 11 rounds across five tracks in three states (9 points rounds and 2 non-points specials). A few subplots worth tracking:

Can anyone beat Jones at Evergreen? The defending champ's home track is the season's first real test after the opener. Jones has rarely lost at Monroe in the last X starts. This weekend is where that streak either grows or breaks.

The Apple Cup pivot. VMRA's mid-season run to Tri-City Raceway for the Apple Cup Classic historically splits the standings. A win there is worth more than a win at the season opener, not in points, in momentum.

The east-side swing. Wenatchee and Stateline in consecutive weekends is the hardest stretch of the schedule. Teams making the round trip from the Seattle side are looking at 12+ hours of tow time and two completely different track styles. Teams that come home in one piece with points in hand usually have a shot at the title.

§ 06 Forty Years, Forty Seasons

The thing that's different about the Class of 2026 isn't the drivers. It's the context. Every green flag this season is the 40th time VMRA has dropped one. That's a rare number in American grassroots motorsports. Most clubs fold before they hit 20. Many never get started at all.

The drivers on this year's grid know that. The ones who have been here the longest talk about it quietly. The rookies are starting to figure it out. And the championship that gets decided in September or October will be the 40th one the club has ever awarded.

Forty years of racing. Forty years of carrying the spirit of 1950s through 1970s modified stock cars forward when nobody else was. Forty years of guys unloading at the same tracks, working on the same cars, telling the same stories at the concession stand.

It's a hell of a grid. Let's go see who wins.

VMRA's 40th Anniversary season is underway. Follow every race recap, preview, and driver profile at vmra.club/news, or come out to one of the tracks on tour. The 2026 championship opened at Tri-City Raceway on April 12 and continues across 11 rounds through September.
