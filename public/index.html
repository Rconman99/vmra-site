About VMRA
Est. 1986 · Pacific Northwest

The Vintage Modified Racing Association (VMRA) is a non-profit Pacific Northwest racing club founded in 1986 at Spanaway Speedway, Washington. We organize vintage modified circle-track races across Washington, Oregon, and Idaho — preserving the spirit of 1950s through early 1970s modified stock car racing through a rulebook built for authenticity and integrity.

The 2026 season is our 40th Anniversary, running at Evergreen Speedway (Monroe WA), Tri-City Raceway (West Richland WA), Wenatchee Valley Super Oval (East Wenatchee WA), South Sound Speedway (Rochester WA), and Stateline Speedway (Post Falls ID).

§ Pre-Race · Round 02 What To Watch For
at Evergreen

Defending Champ Kyten Jones Returns to the Fast Half-Mile With a Target on His Back

Kyten Jones opened the 40th season by taking the 57th Apple Cup at Tri-City — held the lead late on a broken upper control arm. Saturday he heads to the fastest track on the schedule — Evergreen's half-mile paved oval — for Grocery Outlet Night, where Steve Woods has historically found speed. Woods sat P2 in the 2025 final standings. If there's a track where he closes the points gap, this is it.

Stateline's Chad Broom makes the trip over from Idaho carrying momentum from a podium finish at Round 1. Broom has never won at Evergreen — he's chasing his first. Watch the opening 15 laps: if Broom is still top-five, the rest of the field needs to pay attention.

Rookie Shawn Strimple tested at Evergreen in April with the Strimple Racing #37. First real test of the offseason build happens Saturday night. And the 17-car confirmed field means heat races matter — expect strong B-mains.

§ 06 / Answers

Questions, Answered

Full Rulebook →

What is the Vintage Modified Racing Association?

VMRA is a non-profit racing club out of the Pacific Northwest. We started in 1986 at Spanaway Speedway with a small group of guys who wanted to keep 1950s through early '70s modified stock car racing alive. Forty years later we're still at it — running circle-track races at Evergreen, Tri-Cities, Wenatchee, South Sound, and Stateline. American-made pre-1970 cars (with a few specific exceptions), small-block V8s, fiberglass bodies, hand-built in members' shops.

How do I join VMRA or race a vintage modified?

Annual membership is $50. Download the 2026 Membership Application (PDF), print it, fill it out, sign it, and bring it to the next race weekend with cash or a check made out to VMRA. Or email the board at board@vmra.club with questions. Your car needs to pass tech against the construction rules — pre-1970 American body, 2,950 lb minimum, 370 cubic inches max, no fuel injection, no blowers, no coil-overs. Tires are 10-inch-wide Hoosier take-offs (ST1 left, ST2/ST3 right), procured by VMRA. Show up, walk the paddock, talk to people. That's how everyone gets in.

What type of cars race in VMRA?

Vintage modified stock cars — the kind that ran in the '50s, '60s, and early '70s on dirt and pavement ovals across the country. Any American body 1969 or older, plus some 1970-77 compacts (Vega, Pinto, Gremlin) with tech approval. 370 cubic inches max, steel block only, flat-top pistons, carbureted only — no fuel injection, no blowers, no turbos, no nitrous, no alcohol. 2,950 lb minimum with 57% left-side max. 102″ minimum wheelbase. GM 604 sealed crate motors are allowed with no weight break (602 gets a −100 lb break). Real cars built in real shops, not high-dollar spec cars trucked in from somewhere. Full spec is in the 2026-2028 Construction Rules.
